Recommendations
01
Close the Feedback Loop
Share a summary of key themes and any resulting actions after each cycle of sessions. Participants consistently asked what changed as a result of their input.
02
Increase Session Frequency
Move toward quarterly sessions rather than clustering them in one semester. More touchpoints throughout the year improve continuity and reduce recency bias.
03
Expand Reach Across All Units
FY26 YTD had only 5 feedback respondents. Increase awareness and accessibility of the feedback process to ensure representation across all NDR units.
04
Develop a Visible Action Tracking System
Create a simple, shared document or dashboard that tracks what was raised in sessions and what has been done in response.
05
Introduce Pre-Session Agenda Sharing
Allow participants to submit questions or topics beforehand. This improves the quality of discussion and helps facilitators prepare targeted prompts.
06
Invite Leadership Observers Occasionally
Consider having a senior leader attend select sessions in a listening-only capacity. This signals that feedback is taken seriously at the highest level.
07
Maintain Small Group Formats
The 100% recommendation rate and high participation scores reflect the value of the intimate format. Resist the urge to scale sessions up in size at the expense of psychological safety.
