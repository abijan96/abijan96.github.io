Key Takeaways
+
Universal endorsement. All 74 survey respondents said they would recommend the Engagement Chats and find them beneficial for workplace culture — a remarkably consistent signal of program value across NDR.
↑
Strong attendance recovery. After a sharp dip in FY23 (27.4%), attendance has improved for three straight years: FY24 (31.9%), FY25 (40.5%), FY26 YTD (50.0%). The most recent session (Mar 26, 2026) hit 77.8% — the highest since early FY22.
#
Cross-unit connection is the #1 value driver. The most common theme across 67 "liked" responses is meeting colleagues from other parts of NDR. The small, diverse group format is what makes this work — participants consistently ask to preserve it.
!
Communication gaps are a recurring pain point. Sessions consistently surface the same structural issue: information flowing from IDEA Center, leadership decisions, and D2D arrives too late or incompletely. This is an organizational design issue, not a chat format issue.
"
Top format requests: send questions in advance + follow-up updates. The two most requested improvements across 53 format responses are (1) distribute discussion questions ahead of time, and (2) share what changed as a result of previous chats. Both are low-effort, high-impact improvements.
→
30 sessions, 201 total attendees, 39.6% overall rate. The program has reached 201 unique attendance instances across 507 invitations since March 2022. Maintaining momentum above 50% in FY26 YTD suggests the April–June 2026 sessions will be pivotal for closing the year strong.
